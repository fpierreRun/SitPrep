Step 1/3: Where will you gather if your home is not accessible?

Your privacy and safety is important to us. SitPrep and any other entity do not save, store, or share the information you provide.

  • Be sure to select a place that is accessible and known to everyone, such as a landmark, intersections, or public park. If you have a pet, make sure the location is animal friendly.

Gathering Place

It is a good idea to choose an emergency meeting place for your household in case you are apart during an emergency. Be sure to select a place that is accessible and known to everyone, such as a landmark, intersections, or public park. If you have a pet, make sure the location is animal friendly.

Step 2/3: What will be your evacuation plan?

  • Plan how you will leave and where you and your household will go if evacuation is in place.

  • Find different places you can go in case of an emergency, like a friend's house in another town or a motel. Choose destinations in different directions so you have options during an emergency.

  • If you have pets, make sure you know a place where they can stay because most public shelters only allow service animals.

  • Learn about other ways to leave your area, like different routes or transportation options.

  • Always listen to local officials and remember that you may have to walk for your evacuation route depending on the type of disaster.

Evacuation Route

Evacuation from your home can happen for many kind of emergencies. It is important that you have a plan to evacuate safely and quickly. Plan how will you leave and where you and your household will go if evacuation is in place.

Step 3/3: Who will those in your household call or email during an emergency situation?

Keep in mind it might be easier to make a long-distance call than to call nearby during a disaster.

Emergency Contact

During and emergency it is important to have a contact that all of your household can call or email if they can not contact the head(s) of household during an emergency. Keep in mind it might be easier to make a long distance call than to call nearby during a disaster.
