Our Journey and Mission at SitPrep

In 2020, as the COVID-19 pandemic hit, it became evident how unprepared many people were for emergencies. Witnessing the struggles and scarcity faced by individuals, my friends and I decided to create local Facebook pages to coordinate efforts and help our communities. This experience sparked a realization that there should be a better way to prepare for emergencies.

As a UX designer, I took it upon myself to create a prototype that would assist individuals in crafting a 3-day emergency plan and eventually develop a long-term preparedness strategy. Through continuous learning and honing my coding skills, the prototype evolved, but I didn't feel it was ready until a recent personal experience compelled me to take action.

Picture this: finding yourself stranded in a small town, far away from home, all because of a flat tire. It happened to me not too long ago. Late on a Saturday night, a treacherous pothole left me in need of help. However, with all the tire shops closed, my attempts to find local assistance proved fruitless. Even offering to pay any price didn't yield any results. And you might wonder, "What about the spare tire?" Well, I had taken it out weeks ago to make room in the trunk, compounding the situation.

This incident left me feeling helpless in the following days, despite having the means to purchase a new tire. It brought back memories of the chaotic times during the COVID-19 pandemic when supplies were scarce, and people experienced similar vulnerability. Does that resonate with you?

But here's the silver lining. My incredible sister Kirsten and her husband Josh came to my rescue, getting me back on the road the next day. Their support made all the difference.

Now, here's what I want to share with you. I've been diligently developing a website to help you prepare for emergencies. However, I've always held back from launching it because I wanted it to be perfect for all of you to use. Yet, those few days of being stranded instilled a sense of urgency within me. I realized it's crucial to release the website soon, so you can embrace the preparedness mindset rather than waiting for a crisis to strike before taking action.

At SitPrep, our mission is to empower you to be ready for any emergency. We believe that preparedness is not just a reaction but a proactive approach to safeguarding your well-being and that of your loved ones. Our website will provide you with the tools, resources, and guidance to create a comprehensive emergency plan, ensuring peace of mind and resilience in the face of unforeseen circumstances.

Don't wait for a moment of helplessness. Join us on this journey and let's prepare together.

Remember, SitPrep is here for you.

Warm regards,

Dione
